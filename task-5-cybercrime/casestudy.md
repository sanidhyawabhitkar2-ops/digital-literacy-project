Phishing Attack — Case Study

Phishing is one of the most common forms of cybercrime, in which an attacker disguises themselves as a trusted entity to trick victims into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or banking credentials.

How it typically happens:
A first-year college student, Rahul, received an email appearing to be from his university's IT department. The email stated that his college email account would be suspended within 24 hours unless he verified his credentials immediately. The email contained a link that led to a webpage that looked exactly like the university login portal. Rahul, panicking about losing access to his account, entered his username and password. Within minutes, his email account was compromised, his personal data accessed, and the attacker used his college email to send further phishing emails to his classmates.
Who is targeted: Students, elderly users, and anyone unfamiliar with how institutional communication works. Attackers rely on urgency and fear to bypass rational thinking.

Consequences: Loss of account access, identity theft, financial fraud, and in institutional cases, mass compromise of connected systems.

Source: Based on patterns documented by CERT-In (cert-in.org.in) and the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).
